About Me

I am a PhD student at the Czech Technical University in Prague, where I conduct research in artificial intelligence with a focus on interpretability, graph neural networks, and geometric deep learning. My work explores the intersection of neural and symbolic approaches to create more understandable and trustworthy AI systems.

Currently, I'm particularly interested in developing methods that can improve our understanding of how deep learning models make decisions, especially in the context of graph-structured data and geometric problems. I believe that combining the representational power of neural networks with the interpretability of symbolic reasoning is key to building AI systems that we can trust and understand.

When I'm not researching, you can find me exploring new AI applications, contributing to open-source projects, or sharing insights through talks and presentations.
